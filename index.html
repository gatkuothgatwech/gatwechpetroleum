EXTRACTION PROCESS AND TRANSPORTATION

Upstream Operations: Exploration: Petroleum companies search for oil and gas reserves beneath the Earth's surface. This involves geological surveys, seismic studies, and exploratory drilling. Production: Once a viable reserve is identified, companies extract crude oil and natural gas from wells.
Midstream Operations: Transportation: Crude oil needs to be transported from production sites to refineries. This is often done through pipelines, ships, or trucks. Storage: Petroleum companies maintain storage facilities to store crude oil before it is transported to refineries.
Refining: Crude oil is processed in refineries to produce various petroleum products such as gasoline, diesel, jet fuel, and petrochemicals. Marketing and Distribution: Refined products are then distributed to consumers through a network of gas stations, distributors, and retailers.
